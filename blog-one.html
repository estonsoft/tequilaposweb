Understanding Cash Discount: A Smart Way to Cut Processing Fees

A clear explanation of what a Cash Discount is, how it works, and how it benefits both businesses and customers.

WHAT IS
CASH DISCOUNT?

A Cash Discount is a business-friendly pricing strategy where customers receive a discount when they choose to pay using cash, debit card, or ACH (bank transfer) instead of a credit card.

This method is designed to help merchants recover the cost of credit card processing fees, which can range from 2% to 4% per transaction. Instead of increasing prices across the board, businesses offer an incentive to customers who pay through lower-cost channels.

How does it work with
a POS Solution?

01 Two-Tier Pricing

The system shows card vs cash prices and applies discounts automatically when cash/debit is selected.

02 Auto Application

When cash or debit is chosen, the POS applies the discount—no manual calculation needed.

03 Transparency

Receipts clearly show applied discounts, ensuring full visibility and compliance.

04 Integrated Workflow

Built directly into the POS, making it seamless for staff and easy for customers.

Why is this
important?

01 Zero Fees

Businesses can offset or completely remove costly credit card fees, saving thousands annually.

02 Better Margins

By encouraging lower-cost payment methods, businesses retain more revenue from each sale.

03 Smart Incentives

Customers are incentivized to pay with cash or debit, reducing dependency on card networks.

04 Full Compliance

Pricing is clearly displayed, and discounts are applied in a compliant, friendly way.

Traditional Payment Processing: What It Means for Your Business

A breakdown of how traditional card payments work and why merchants end up paying a percentage of every transaction.

The Cost of Convenience: Understanding
Traditional
Credit Card Processing

Learn how businesses are charged through interchange, processor markups, and hidden fees in the standard payment model.

What is
Traditional
Credit Card Processing?

Traditional processing refers to the standard method where a business accepts credit or debit card payments and pays a processing fee to various parties involved in the transaction (banks, card networks, processors).

In this model, merchants absorb the cost of accepting card payments, meaning a portion of every sale goes to processing fees, reducing overall profit margins.

How Are
Fees Structured?

Interchange Fee (set by card-issuing banks):
  • Charged by the customer's bank.
  • Varies based on card type (credit/debit, rewards cards).
  • Typically 1.5%-2% per transaction.
Assessment Fee (set by card networks like Visa, Mastercard):
  • Fixed percentage for using the card brand's network.
  • Usually 0.13%-0.15% of the transaction.
Processor Markup (charged by your payment processor or POS provider):
  • The profit margin for the processor.
  • Can include a flat fee per transaction + percentage (e.g., ₹0.30 + 0.5%).
